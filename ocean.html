Introduction

The ocean is the vast body of salt water that covers about 70.8% of Earth’s surface. It is usually divided into five major oceans: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic. These oceans are further divided into smaller bodies of water, such as seas, gulfs, and bays.

The ocean holds about 97% of all the water on Earth, making it a vital part of the planet’s water system. It also helps regulate Earth’s climate by storing heat, moving carbon, and influencing weather patterns around the world.

Beyond shaping the climate, the ocean is essential for life. It is home to countless animals, plants, and tiny organisms, and it plays a major role in producing the oxygen we breathe. In fact, ocean life is responsible for about half of Earth’s oxygen.
